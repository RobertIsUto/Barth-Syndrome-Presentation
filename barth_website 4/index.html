Barth Syndrome Background

BTHS– X-linked Mitochondrial Disorder

A. Disease physiological effects description and symptoms

  • Cardiomyopathy – dilated or hypertrophic heart
  • Skeletal myopathy – muscle weakness, fatigue
  • Neutropenia – low white blood cells, infections
  • Growth delay – short stature, delayed puberty

B. Disease prevalence

  • 1 in 300,000–400,000 live births.
  • ~230–250 males identified globally. Primarily affects males.

C. Diagnostic Techniques

  • Urinalysis — 3-methylglutaconic acid levels
  • Echocardiography — heart function
  • Genetic testing — TAFAZZIN mutations

Barth Syndrome Cells

A. What tissues are involved in the disease?

  • Cardiac muscle (myocardium)
  • Skeletal muscle
  • Bone marrow, Immune system

B. What cell types are involved in the disease?

  • Cardiomyocytes, Myocytes
  • Neutrophils, Myeloid progenitors

C. What happens to cells (pathological changes)?

  • Abnormal mitochondrial structure
  • Impaired oxidative phosphorylation
  • Cristae disorganization

What Genes Are Mutated in Barth Syndrome?

A. What genes and mutations cause the disease?

  • TAFAZZIN (TAZ): Phospholipid-lysophospholipid transacylase that remodels cardiolipin in the mitochondrial inner membrane, enhancing mitochondrial function and influencing the respiratory chain; located on Xq28.
  • Gene: ~10,000 base pairs, 11 exons
  • >160 mutations identified
  • Types: missense, nonsense, deletions
  • Example Mutations:
    • c.280C>T (p.Arg94Cys)
    • Exon deletions, Splice variants

Sources:

Saric et al. Front Genet. 2016

Barth Syndrome Pathways

  • CLS1 synthesizes immature cardiolipin (CL) in the inner mitochondrial membrane.
  • iPLA2γ removes acyl chains from immature CL to form monolysocardiolipin (MLCL).
  • TAFAZZIN reacylates MLCL with linoleic acid from PC/PE donors to form mature tetralinoleoyl-CL.
  • Mature CL stabilizes respiratory chain supercomplexes (I, III, IV) and ATP synthase.
  • CL maintains cristae structure through interactions with OPA1.
  • In BTHS: mutant tafazzin causes MLCL accumulation and CL deficiency.

Barth Syndrome Cure

A. What molecular tools would be used to correct the gene (from slide 3)?

  • AAV9 vector carrying functional human TAFAZZIN gene (gene replacement therapy)

B. What would you do to the gene/DNA (what specific modifications would be made)?

  • Deliver a new functional copy of TAFAZZIN with desmin promoter for cardiac/skeletal muscle expression
  • Gene remains as episome in nucleus (does not integrate into host genome)

C. How would this therapy be delivered to the cells and how would it target the specific cells (from slide 2)?

  • Intravenous (IV) injection of AAV9-hTAZ vector
  • AAV9 has natural tropism for cardiac and skeletal muscle tissue
